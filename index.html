Uncovering the Clues: Signs of Pain

Listen for these verbal red flags. They are direct indicators of operational friction and a strong opportunity for a Simpro solution.

Operational Bottlenecks

  • "We still use paper forms or spreadsheets..."
  • "We don't know if a job is profitable until a month later."
  • "Our techs have to call the office multiple times a day."
  • "We have a lot of manual data entry slowing us down."

Financial Disconnect

  • "Invoices get held up because techs forget to log materials."
  • "We have to write off a lot of time on big projects."
  • "We've struggled with cash flow because of slow billing."

Contract Risk

  • "It's a struggle to track all the assets we maintain."
  • "Our renewal process is reactive and catches us off guard."
  • "We've had client complaints about missed deadlines."

The Handoff: From Clue to Case

A successful handoff is a structured process. Gather the right intel, build confidence in the AE, and provide a clear, concise summary to ensure a successful next step.

Qualitative Details

Capture the story and the context. This is the 'why' behind their pain.

  • Prospect's specific language
  • Names and roles of stakeholders
  • A recent, specific challenge
  • Current software/process frustrations

Quantifiable Information

Attach numbers to the pain. This is the 'how much' that drives urgency.

  • Number of techs or contracts
  • Approximate contract value
  • Estimated time or money lost
  • Key deadlines (e.g., renewals)

1. Gather Intel

Collect both qualitative and quantitative data.

2. Sell the Salesperson

Build confidence by highlighting the AE's expertise and experience.

3. Deliver Handoff Script

Provide a concise, pain-focused summary to the AE.
